Origins in Calamba

José was born on June 19, 1861 in Calamba, Laguna, into a family that valued education, faith, and justice. His parents, Francisco Mercado and Teodora Alonzo, nurtured his love for books, languages, and the arts.

From childhood, Rizal showed a strong spirit and curiosity. He learned Spanish, Tagalog, Latin, and later German and French. The lush landscapes and social struggles of his hometown planted the seeds of his lifelong mission.

A Young Mind Awakens

By his teenage years, Rizal had already written poems, sketched landscapes, and questioned the world around him. His early experiences later became the foundation for his novels and reform ideas.

Rizal's family was well-educated; his father was a tenant farmer who valued learning, and his mother taught him to read and write. This nurturing environment fostered his intellectual curiosity and moral character.

As a boy, he witnessed the injustices of colonial rule, including the execution of Filipino priests in 1872, which deeply influenced his views on reform and justice.

Early Education and Talents

Rizal began his formal education at the age of 9, attending a private school in Biñan. He excelled in academics, showing exceptional talent in languages, mathematics, and the arts.

His early writings and drawings revealed a creative spirit that would later manifest in his literary works and sculptures. These formative years instilled in him a love for his country and a commitment to social change.
